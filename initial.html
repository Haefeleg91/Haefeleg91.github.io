The initial design of the LFO

The initial design of the robot, or LFO, was fairly basic

The start of the project was small and I started with the arms and lower legs first because they were all uniform

The initial parts were not that hard to make, they were mostly basic geometry with the most challenging piece being the spike on the knee cap of the lower leg. All the parts actually started as a block at about the size that I wanted and I cut away what I didn't want, very similar to sculpting, the upper arm didn't start like this though. The upper arm was special, it started as a rough sketch of what I wanted and I revolved that sketch around the center axis, I could do it for this one because it was a cylindrical part, I then went in and created the needed holes for the other pieces to socket into.

Next I moved onto something a little more difficult, the upper legs

These took a little while mostly because they were not symmetrical. The way I ended up making these is making the overall block without the cutout for the hip. I then saved it as two different copies, one for the left and one for the right and then cut out the socket for the hip on the respective side.

The final pieces I made were the body, waist, and joints

The body was the biggest undertaking in the project. It started as a solid block and I cut away from it to eventually make it into what it became. The biggest issue that I had with it was seating the cockpit canopy on the body. Not much worked and what I ended up doing was locking the center axis of the canopy and the hole that I made for it. The canopy is uniform around a center axis so it's actually offset to one side but it's below the body so it's not noticeable. This was actually the third iteration of the body. The first one was curvy and had smooth lines but it wasn't fitting the rest of the design, the second was similar to the first except it didn't taper to the nose and was mostly a rectangle.
